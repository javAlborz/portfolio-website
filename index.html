My Journey

Navigating the dynamic intersection of software engineering and applied AI, I've immersed myself in the evolving role of the AI Engineer. As we've moved into 2023, it's evident how the AI landscape has shifted; tasks that were once the domain of researchers are now accessible to AI professionals like myself. My journey began during my BSc in Electrical Engineering, where I delved into Robotic Software Architecture and Control Systems Design, focusing on low-level programming and system integration. I then transitioned into an MSc in Autonomous Systems Engineering at DTU, further broadening my expertise with a keen focus on computer vision, image analysis, and data mining.

The AI ecosystem of today has allowed me to transition between tweaking models and building solutions using APIs, merging traditional programming techniques with the capabilities of modern AI tools. Whether it's evaluating powerful proprietary models or leveraging open-source options, I've positioned myself to harness their potential for practical applications. Integrating machine learning models with human-written code, I aim to produce efficient and impactful solutions. In this evolving landscape, my objective is not just to keep up but to be a proactive contributor, turning advancements into beneficial, tangible applications.
